About Compound Interest

Compound interest is the interest on a loan or deposit calculated based on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. It's often described as "interest on interest."

The Power of Compound Interest

Compound interest can significantly increase your savings over time. The earlier you start saving, the more time your money has to grow. Even small amounts can grow substantially over long periods due to compounding.

Compound Interest Formula

For a single initial investment:

A = P(1 + r/n)nt

Where:

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal (initial investment)
  • r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
  • n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Time in years

The Rule of 72

The Rule of 72 is a simple way to determine how long an investment will take to double given a fixed annual rate of interest. Divide 72 by the annual rate of return to get the approximate number of years it will take for the investment to double.

Years to double = 72 / Interest Rate (%)

Tips for Maximizing Compound Interest

  • Start early: The earlier you start investing, the more time your money has to grow.
  • Be consistent: Regular contributions can significantly increase your returns.
  • Reinvest dividends: Reinvesting dividends allows your investment to compound faster.
  • Increase contributions over time: As your income grows, consider increasing your contributions.
  • Be patient: Compound interest works best over long periods of time.
